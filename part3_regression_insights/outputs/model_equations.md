\# **Model Equations**



\## Simple Regression Model 1



Dependent Variable: Monthly Sales



Independent Variable: Marketing Spend



Regression Equation:



Monthly Sales = 560777.3 + (2.1296 × Marketing Spend)



\### Business Interpretation



For every one-unit increase in marketing spend, monthly sales increase by approximately 2.13 units on average.



\---



\## Simple Regression Model 2



Dependent Variable: Monthly Sales



Independent Variable: Footfall



Regression Equation:



Monthly Sales = 446410.6 + (35.6780 × Footfall)



\### Business Interpretation



For every additional customer visiting the store, monthly sales increase by approximately 35.68 units on average.

\## Multiple Regression Model



Dependent Variable: Monthly Sales

Monthly Sales =

129781

\+ (1.179 × Marketing Spend)

\+ (33.528 × Footfall)

\+ (2969.11 × Inventory Availability %)

\+ (25645.7 × Airport Dummy)



\### Business Interpretation of Coefficients



\- Marketing Spend has a positive relationship with monthly sales.

\- Higher Footfall is associated with higher monthly sales.

\- Better Inventory Availability is associated with higher monthly sales.

\- Airport stores are expected to generate higher monthly sales than Residential stores (the reference category), holding all other variables constant.



\---



\## Dummy Variable Explanation



The categorical variable \*\*Store Type\*\* was converted into dummy variables.



Three dummy variables were created:



\- Airport Dummy

\- High Street Dummy

\- Mall Dummy



Residential was selected as the \*\*reference category\*\* and therefore was not included in the regression model. This avoids the dummy variable trap caused by perfect multicollinearity.



\---



\## Final Model Selected



The Multiple Regression Model was selected as the final model because it achieved the highest R² value (0.809), included multiple important business drivers, and provided the strongest explanation of monthly sales.



It is the most suitable model for supporting business decisions.
