\# **Final Recommendation**



\## Executive Summary



A regression analysis was conducted to identify the factors most strongly associated with monthly sales across retail stores. Three models were evaluated, including two simple regression models and one multiple regression model.



The multiple regression model performed the best, explaining approximately 80.9% of the variation in monthly sales (R² = 0.809).



\---



\## Key Findings



The variables most strongly associated with monthly sales are:



\- Footfall

\- Marketing Spend

\- Inventory Availability

\- Airport Store Type



All of these variables showed positive relationships with monthly sales and were statistically significant in the final model.



\---



\## Variables Leadership Should Focus On



Leadership should prioritize:



\- Increasing customer footfall through marketing and promotions.

\- Investing marketing budgets where they generate the highest return.

\- Maintaining high inventory availability to reduce lost sales.

\- Understanding why airport stores perform better and applying similar strategies where possible.



\---



\## Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted



Although all variables were statistically significant, regression analysis identifies relationships rather than direct cause-and-effect. External factors such as local competition, seasonality, economic conditions, and customer preferences may also influence sales.



\---



\## Business Recommendation



The multiple regression model should be used to support business planning and decision-making. Leadership should focus on increasing customer traffic, maintaining inventory availability, and optimizing marketing investment to improve monthly sales performance.



\---



\## Risks and Limitations



This analysis is based only on the variables available in the dataset.



Important business factors such as local competition, pricing strategies, seasonal demand, and economic conditions were not included.



Regression models provide useful predictions but should always be combined with business judgment.



\---



\## Association vs Causation



Regression analysis identifies statistical associations between variables and monthly sales. However, these relationships do not automatically prove that one variable directly causes changes in sales. Business experiments or additional analysis would be required to establish causation.
